Banjarmasin was founded on September 24, 1526, by Sultan Suriansyah as the first king of the Banjar Sultanate. The city is known for its rich cultural heritage. Its traditional houses are called "Rumah Banjar Bubungan Tinggi" (high-sided houses), with traditional dances such as "Baksa Kembang" and traditional songs such as "Ampar-Ampar Pisang" and "Paris Barantai".

Furthermore, the city is also known as the "City of a Thousand Rivers" due to the numerous rivers that flow through it. One of these is the Barito River, the longest river in South Kalimantan. Beyond its rich culture and traditions, the city also boasts popular tourist attractions and culinary delights, including the floating market (Pasar Terapung).
